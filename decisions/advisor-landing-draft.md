# Meet Advisor

## Your work, handled by a team that never loses the plot

Most AI tools give you one assistant juggling everything at once — your question, your files, your follow-ups — until it starts forgetting things and cutting corners. Advisor takes a different approach. It puts one sharp coordinator in charge, and that coordinator hands each piece of work to a dedicated specialist who does one thing, does it properly, and reports back.

You describe what you want. Advisor figures out the rest.

## The core idea: a chief of staff, not a lone assistant

Think of Advisor as a chief of staff.

When you bring a chief of staff a big request — "find out whether we should switch suppliers" — they don't disappear into a library for three days. They break the request into clear assignments, hand each one to the right person, check the work as it comes back, and return to you with one coherent answer.

That's exactly what Advisor does. It reads your request, splits it into well-defined tasks, and summons a specialist for each one: a researcher, a planner, a code reviewer, a writer. Each specialist gets a precise assignment — what to do, what the finished result should look like, and what's explicitly *not* their job. They work in their own clean space, deliver their result, and step away. The Advisor then weaves everything into a single answer for you.

No single helper trying to hold your entire project in its head. A coordinated team, each member doing what they're best at.

## What you can do with it

**Get real research, not a quick skim.** Ask a big question and Advisor sends out multiple researchers, each covering a different angle, with sources cited for every meaningful claim. You get a grounded report instead of a confident guess.

**Have your code reviewed by a dedicated critic.** A reviewer whose only job is finding problems looks at your work — it doesn't write code, so it has no incentive to go easy on itself.

**Turn a fuzzy goal into a concrete plan.** The planner breaks a project into ordered steps, flags what can happen in parallel, and defines what "done" means for each piece — before anyone touches anything.

**Get bugs fixed with proof.** The coder doesn't just say "fixed." It first demonstrates the bug, then shows the fix making it go away — evidence included, every time.

**Run a competition for the hardest problems.** When there's more than one way to solve something, Advisor can have several specialists each build their own version, using genuinely different approaches — then put every entry through the same set of tests and keep the winner. The best solution is chosen by results, not by opinion.

**Get documents and pages written for you.** Reports, write-ups, web pages, specs — handled by specialists who deliver finished files, not rough drafts you have to rescue.

The common thread: you ask once, in plain language, and the result that comes back is complete.

## How it works, simply

Three steps, every time.

1. **Summon.** You describe what you want. Advisor turns it into one or more precise assignments and calls up the right specialist for each.
2. **Work.** Each specialist works independently in its own dedicated space, focused entirely on its one assignment. You can watch the whole team work in real time — every helper side by side on one screen, plus a live dashboard where each update appears the moment it happens — or simply wait for the answer.
3. **Report.** Each specialist hands back its finished work. Advisor checks it, pulls everything together, and gives you one clear answer with the finished files attached.

If something needs another pass — "make it shorter," "now cover the budget side" — Advisor simply sends a fresh specialist to refine it. You never have to manage any of this yourself.

## Why it's built this way

**Focus produces better work.** A specialist with one clear assignment and nothing else on its mind does sharper work than a generalist juggling ten threads. Every assignment comes with explicit boundaries, so no one drifts off-topic or duplicates someone else's effort.

**No overload.** AI assistants degrade when they're asked to hold too much at once — details blur, instructions slip. Advisor keeps every helper's workload small and clean: one task, one workspace, one result. The coordinator stays clear-headed too, because it delegates the heavy lifting instead of doing it all itself.

**Nothing gets forgotten.** After every piece of work comes back, Advisor records what was established and what questions remain — in writing, not just in memory. Long projects don't dissolve into "wait, what did we decide?" The record is always there. And when an assistant's working memory eventually fills up — as all AI memory does — Advisor saves its place first and picks up exactly where it left off. A reset is a pause, not a restart.

## Your work is never lost

This is the promise underneath everything else: nothing you've built gets thrown away.

Specialists work in their own private workspaces, and those workspaces get tidied up when the job is done. But before anything is cleaned away — by anyone, for any reason — every file in it is copied to permanent storage first. If that copy can't be made, the cleanup simply refuses to happen. Deletion waits for safekeeping, never the other way around.

The automatic janitor that sweeps up old, abandoned workspaces knows the difference between clutter and fresh work. Anything just getting started, or still being set up, is left untouched — there's no race between cleanup and a specialist that only began a moment ago.

And if something crashes mid-task, the system heals itself: no stuck files, no half-written records, no wedged sessions. Should a session ever stall anyway, a built-in check-up examines it and reports exactly where things stand — so you're diagnosing in seconds, not guessing.

## What sets it apart

**Parallel helpers.** Independent tasks run at the same time, not one after another. Ask a question with five angles, and five researchers start at once — each with its own assigned territory so they never cover the same ground twice.

**The right brainpower for each job.** Not every task deserves the heaviest thinking. Advisor dials each specialist's intelligence to fit the assignment — quick and economical for routine work, stepping up through the ranks to Fable 5, the most capable model available, for the problems that genuinely need it. Top-tier thinking where it counts, none wasted where it doesn't.

**A memory that gets smarter with every project.** Most AI assistants wake up with amnesia: every hard-won discovery, every dead end, every "we already tried that" vanishes when the session ends — and the next session pays to learn it all again. Advisor refuses to lose that knowledge. When an approach fails, the failure is written into a permanent vault as a concrete rule, and that rule is handed to the next specialist who takes on similar work — before it starts, not after it stumbles. It also remembers what past projects established and where they left off, so a familiar task begins from what's already known instead of from zero. The result: the same mistake doesn't happen twice, and the whole system genuinely improves the more you use it.

**It sees your codebase as a whole, not one file at a time.** Reading files one by one is how most tools work — and it's how subtle problems slip through, because a change in one file quietly breaks something three files away. Advisor can build a living map of how everything in your codebase connects: what calls what, what depends on what. Reviewers use that map to catch ripple effects a file-by-file reader would never spot — the function that looks safe to change until you see who relies on it, the code that nothing uses anymore, the migration that touches more than it appears to. You find out before the change ships, not after.

**Marathon projects don't choke on their own history.** A long project generates an enormous internal paper trail — drafts, status updates, back-and-forth between specialists. Left unchecked, all that history becomes dead weight: slow to wade through and expensive to keep re-reading. Advisor periodically compresses the repetitive chatter while keeping every decision, conclusion, and record that matters — like turning a week of meeting transcripts into clean minutes. So the team stays fast on day ten of a project the way it was on day one, and nothing important is ever compressed away.

**Built-in double-checking.** Results don't just get accepted. An evaluator can score each deliverable for accuracy, completeness, and source quality — and if the work falls short, Advisor sends it back for another round before you ever see it. Claims about prices, licenses, and product versions get an extra fact-checking pass against authoritative sources.

## The short version

Advisor is a chief of staff for your AI: it breaks your request into focused assignments, matches each one with the right level of intelligence, runs them through dedicated specialists — in parallel where possible — lets you watch it all live, double-checks the results, keeps every file safe no matter what, learns from every project, and hands you one finished answer.

You bring the goal. It brings the team.
